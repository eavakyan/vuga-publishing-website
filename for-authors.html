This isn't a press release. It's a full editorial article.

A press release is a corporate announcement that gets distributed to a wire and ignored. An editorial article is a real story, written by an editor, published on a real magazine page. They're not the same product — and the difference is everything.

Press Release Distribution

  • Wire-service blast — the same boilerplate copy pushed to PR Newswire, EIN, GlobeNewswire, then auto-syndicated to no-traffic news aggregators
  • Reads like an ad — third-person announcement format, "[Author] is pleased to announce…" — readers skip it on sight
  • No editorial vetting — anyone with a credit card can publish; carries zero authority
  • Lives on syndication clones — Yahoo Finance shadow-pages, Benzinga partner feeds — not on a flagship magazine
  • Often de-indexed — Google treats wire-syndicated copy as duplicate content; rank value collapses fast
  • $300–$2,000 for a single distribution — and you still don't get a single real magazine feature

VUGA Editorial Article

  • Original written feature — 600–1,200 words, written by an editor, structured as a real story with hook, narrative, and reader value
  • Reads like real journalism — published in the magazine's editorial voice, not a corporate announcement
  • Lives on the actual magazine site — closerweekly.com, intouchweekly.com, starmagazine.com, okmagazine.com, hollywoodlife.com, time.com, rollingstone.co.uk
  • Permanent URL on a tier-1 domain — DA 80+ backlink to your Amazon page, indexed by Google, surfaces in author searches forever
  • Editorially placed by contract — guaranteed slot, no pitching, no "we'll try"
  • The product professional publicists charge $25K+ to attempt — and they still can't guarantee it lands

FAQ

Common questions.

Is the placement actually guaranteed?

Yes — by contract. VUGA holds long-standing editorial access to Closer Weekly, In Touch Weekly, Star Magazine, OK! Magazine, and the 104-outlet VUGA Enterprises network. When you buy a package, the articles go live in the listed outlets, period. No pitching. No "we'll try."

Do I have to talk to anyone?

For Spark ($997) and Bestseller ($7,997): no. Pure self-service — pick a tier, pay online, fill the book-info form. Articles run automatically.

For Authority ($14,997) and the TIME / Rolling Stone UK add-ons: a short intro call is available on request — these are large purchases and many authors want to talk through the scope before sending payment. The call is optional. If you'd rather skip it and buy direct, that works too.

Do I have to be a VUGA Publishing author?

No. The packages work for any indie author — KDP, IngramSpark, Lulu, BookBaby, or any other publisher. Just give us your retailer link in the order form.

What goes in the article?

Each article is a full editorial piece — typically 600–1,200 words — built around your book's hook, the author angle, and reader value. We write it from the synopsis and book info you provide. You see proofs and approve before publication.

What genres work?

The four magazines cover celebrity, lifestyle, relationships, beauty, parenting, and pop-culture topics. Books that translate easily into those angles see the strongest pickup: memoirs, self-help, lifestyle, women's fiction, dating/relationships, parenting, beauty, true crime, celebrity-adjacent non-fiction. Hard sci-fi, technical, and academic books are not a fit.

What's the difference between an editorial article and a press release?

A press release is a wire-service blast — boilerplate, written in third-person announcement format, syndicated to no-traffic aggregators, often de-indexed by Google as duplicate content. It costs $300–$2,000 and gets you no real magazine feature.

A VUGA editorial article is a full original story — 600–1,200 words written by an editor — published on the actual magazine's domain (closerweekly.com, time.com, rollingstone.co.uk, etc.). Permanent URL, real journalism voice, DA 80+ backlink. This is the product professional publicists charge $25K+ to attempt — and they still can't guarantee placement.
