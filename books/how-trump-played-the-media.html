How Trump Played the Media… Again

The insider's playbook on modern political campaigning, media narrative control, and winning elections in a digital-first world. By Gene Avakyan.

Political Non-Fiction

A how-to manual on running a winning campaign.

The ultimate insider's guide to how Donald Trump once again controlled the media narrative and reshaped modern political campaigning. Packed with analysis, tactical breakdowns, and real-world strategies, this book reveals the secrets behind his influence, the so-called "Red Wave," and the new rules of winning elections in a digital-first world.

Whether you're a political strategist, journalist, or just someone who wants to understand the game, this is the must-read playbook for elections in the modern media landscape.

Inside you'll learn:

  • How to control a media cycle from a single tweet, statement, or rally
  • Why traditional polling and pundits keep getting it wrong
  • The mechanics of viral political messaging
  • How a candidate's brand identity becomes the campaign
  • Tactical breakdowns of decisive moments in recent elections
  • The new rulebook for digital-first political communication
